Blood-to-Tissue Translation in Autoimmune Disease: Paired Single-Cell Evidence from Systemic Sclerosis
Niket Rajeevan, Zain Khan
Background & Motivation
  • Tissue-specific biology drives progression and therapy response in autoimmune diseases like SSc
  • Direct tissue sampling (e.g., skin biopsy) is invasive and not feasible for longitudinal monitoring
  • Peripheral blood is routinely sampled but its ability to reflect tissue molecular state is unclear
  • Key question: Can blood measurements enable computational inference of tissue state?

Motivates the setup: Blood and skin cells separate cleanly because they are different tissues with different resident populations, and biological organization persists across the compartmental boundary. Immune cells traffic between blood and tissue and acquire transcriptional imprints of the environments they transit; systemic inflammatory mediators reconfigure both compartments in parallel through soluble signaling. Both mechanisms are sufficient to produce cross compartment alignment, and they predict a bridge that spans beyond any single inflammatory axis.
Study Overview & Key Results
  • Paired blood and skin scRNA-seq from 74 individuals (57 SSc, 17 controls)
  • Assessed 63 pathways: 43 significant blood–skin associations (FDR < 0.05)
  • 212 cross-compartment cell-type associations after correcting for disease status and sex
  • Predictive classifiers recover tissue programs from blood with AUCs up to 0.79
  • Paired autoencoder learns shared latent blood–tissue geometry, enabling tissue-state inference from blood
Data: Paired Single-Cell RNA-Seq
  • Bulk RNA-seq averages out crucial cellular heterogeneity
  • Single-cell RNA-seq preserves distribution over 53 annotated cell states
  • Gur et al. cohort: 74 paired blood/skin samples, 192,809 cells
  • Sufficient sample size to estimate patient-level features and test cross-compartment associations
Hierarchical Blood Representation
  • Blood represents tissue state at multiple biological scales:
  • 1. Pseudobulk (aggregate expression)
  • 2. Cell-type proportions (compositional features)
  • 3. Gene-set scores (pathway activities, both whole-blood and cell-type specific)
  • 4. Single-cell distributional features (variance, high-activation fraction, percentile scores)
  • Compressed, robust representation to match current cohort size
Tissue-Defined Targets
  • Tissue targets from skin: gene-set scores and cell-state proportions
  • Binary molecular phenotypes: e.g., tissue-interferon-high, fibrosis-high, fibroblast subtype programs (COMP, POSTN, MYOC2, etc.)
  • Labels based only on tissue; blood data not used in label construction
  • Core test: recover tissue subpopulation states from blood
Cross-Compartment Structure: Interferon as Validation
  • Type I interferon programs are active in both blood and tissue in SSc
  • Strong cross-compartment correlation for interferon gene-set scores (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.59–0.60)
  • Signal persists within SSc patients, not just case-control difference
  • Confirms that blood captures disease-relevant tissue programs

Shows the best out-of-fold AUC achieved by any blood representation for each tissue target. AUCs range from 0.62 to 0.79. The strongest recoveries are on tissue programs with no direct circulating analogue. Fibroblast COMP reaches 0.79 from blood cell-type proportions. COMP is a cartilage-matrix gene expressed by a specific dermal fibroblast subtype; while serum COMP is elevated in SSc as a bulk fibrosis proxy [13], fibroblasts themselves do not circulate, and the identity of the fibroblast subpopulation producing the program is resolved only in tissue. What proportions capture is the compositional signature that a COMP-active tissue environment leaves on peripheral immune architecture. Fibroblast COCH (0.752) and fibroblast MYOC2 (0.742) follow the same pattern, both recovered from proportions. Fibroblast POSTN reaches 0.737 from single-cell distributional features rather than proportions, a distinction examined in section 4.2. DC-state reaches 0.739 from whole-blood pseudobulk; plasma cell programs reach 0.717 from proportions, as expected for a population that circulates as plasmablasts before tissue homing [14]. Tissue interferon reaches 0.709 from gene-set mean scores — the aggregate pathway score across all blood cells — because interferon elevates the same transcriptional program across multiple blood cell types simultaneously [6] and decomposing it into per-cell-type structure adds marginal information.
Predictive Modeling Protocol
  • Patients labeled tissue-high/low by median split of skin scores
  • Random forest classifier predicts tissue label from blood alone
  • Repeated stratified K-fold CV (5 folds × 2 repeats), all feature engineering inside training folds
  • Evaluation at every blood representation level (pseudobulk, proportions, gene-set, single-cell, combined)
Per-Target Tissue State Recovery
  • Best blood-based AUCs for tissue programs: 0.62–0.79
  • Strongest: fibroblast COMP (0.79), COCH (0.75), MYOC2 (0.74), POSTN (0.74)
  • Notable: these fibroblast programs have no direct blood analog
  • Other tissue states (immune activation, monocyte subtypes, stromal T cells) also recoverable above chance

This supports the potential for non-invasive blood-based inference of tissue states in autoimmune disease monitoring and clinical trials.

Single-Cell Distributional Feature Extraction
  • For each patient, within each cell type and gene set:
  • --- Compute mean, variance, high-activation fraction, 90th percentile
  • --- Concatenate across all eligible cell types and gene sets to form fixed-length patient vector
  • Enables modeling of within-population heterogeneity and rare subpopulation activity

Paired Autoencoder for Cross-Compartment Inference
  • Paired multiview autoencoder trained on both blood and tissue patient representations
  • Training objectives: paired reconstruction, contrastive alignment, neighborhood preservation, tissue-target supervision
  • Learns shared latent geometry: blood-only projections map to tissue-state space
  • Supports improved recovery of held-out tissue targets over simpler baselines
Paired Autoencoder Objective Function
\[\mathcal{L} _ {\mathrm{rec}} = \mathcal{L} _ {\mathrm{self}} + \gamma \mathcal{L} _ {\mathrm{cross}} , \qquad \gamma = 0 . 2 5 ,\]
\[\mathcal{L} _ {\mathrm{var}} = \frac {1} {2 K} \sum _ {k} \left[ \operatorname*{max} ( 0 , 1 - \mathrm{sd} ( z _ {\cdot k} ^ {( k , \mathrm{raw} )} ) ) + \operatorname*{max} ( 0 , 1 - \mathrm{sd} ( z _ {\cdot k} ^ {( t , \mathrm{raw} )} ) ) \right] .\]
\[\begin{array} {r} {\mathcal{L} _ {\mathrm{nbr}} = \frac {1} {2} \Big [ \mathrm{MSE} _ {\mathrm{offdiag}} \Big ( K ( V ^ {( b )} ) , K ( Z ^ {( b )} ) \Big ) + \mathrm{MSE} _ {\mathrm{offdiag}} \Big ( K ( V ^ {( t )} ) , K ( Z ^ {( t )} ) \Big ) \Big ] ,} \end{array}\]
\[\begin{array} {r} {\mathcal{L} _ {\mathrm{breg}} = \mathrm{Huber} ( \hat {y} _ {i} ^ {( b )} , \tilde {y} _ {i} ) , \qquad \mathcal{L} _ {\mathrm{treg}} = \mathrm{Huber} ( \hat {y} _ {i} ^ {( t )} , \tilde {y} _ {i} ) ,} \end{array}\]
These equations define the loss functions for the multiview autoencoder: reconstruction, variance regularization, neighborhood preservation, and supervised (Bregman) loss. Together they guide the model to align and reconstruct both blood and tissue representations in a shared latent space.

shows the held-out quantitative result. The blood-side latent readout is evaluated on continuous tissue targets under patient-level cross-validation and compared against the best simple baseline on the same patients and folds. The comparison metric is mean held-out Spearman correlation. The gray interval is a conservative null band spanning the upper control range from the target-permuted and shuffled-pair training-time null families. On several held-out tissue targets the latent readout exceeds both the best simple baseline and the null band, indicating that paired multiview training extracts information from blood that is not captured by the same patient vectors under simpler linear readouts or under broken pairing/supervision controls. Illustrative examples include skin LC, fibroblast MYOC2 proportion, and tissue fatty-acid metabolism in the all-paired setting, and UV response up, vascular ACKR1 proportion, and mitotic spindle within SSc.

Key Takeaways
  • Peripheral blood contains a recoverable projection of tissue molecular state in SSc
  • Cross-compartment structure is multidimensional and biologically interpretable
  • Fibroblast programs with no blood analog can be inferred from blood features
  • Single-cell and distributional features are essential for optimal inference
  • Paired autoencoder framework enables cross-compartment translation and outperforms simpler approaches
