Backprop as local chain rule

Each neuron only needs two things during the backward pass: the gradient coming in from downstream, and its own local derivative.

Teach the pattern before teaching every symbol.

  • start at the loss
  • move one edge backward
  • multiply by the local slope
\( \frac{dL}{dz} = \frac{dL}{da} \cdot \frac{da}{dz} \)
Teaching line
Incoming gradient times local derivative gives the outgoing gradient.

Output-layer example

\( \frac{dL}{dw_j} = \frac{dL}{dz} a_j \)
Interpretation
A weight gets a large gradient when a large activation carries a large downstream error signal.
